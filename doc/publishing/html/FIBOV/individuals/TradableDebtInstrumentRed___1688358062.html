Annotations (10)

  • explanatoryNote "Debt represented by a tradable debt security may be paid as a one off payment at maturity, by paying down principal amounts periodically, or by a call of the issue by the issuer. Debt that is defined by a convertible bond are redeemed by conversion into an equity security issued by the same company. More notes from the EDMC SME Review: Distinction between call and amortizing is whether you pay off everybody at the same time or via a lottery. SF can be either. either amortize the payment of a SF or pay it off by a lottery. The terms of the instrument determine that. Mandatory - all in the schedule. Mandatory to the bond issuer. Issuer has to pay off a certain amount at a certain time - this is in the Schedule. Callable - nothing mandatory." ()
